# PYRAXIS — Experience Blueprint
**Manual 05 of 05 · Version 1.0**

---

## 01 · Experience Philosophy

The website is not divided into sections. It is divided into scenes.

Each scene continues the previous one. The visitor never feels they entered another page. The experience behaves like one continuous intelligent system.

Scrolling controls time, not position. The visitor doesn't move down a page. They move through a story.

Every scene inherits: particles · lighting · motion · camera · atmosphere · rhythm. Nothing resets. Everything evolves.

---

## 02 · Motion Principles

**Nothing appears. Everything transforms.**

| Prohibited | Preferred |
|------------|-----------|
| Fade | Construct |
| Pop | Manufacture |
| Slide | Flow |
| Zoom | Morph |
| Bounce | Merge / Split |
| Spin randomly | Assemble / Disassemble |

Motion should feel physically believable. Every object has mass, momentum, weight, friction, and inertia. Nothing teleports. Nothing suddenly appears. Everything travels.

**Motion must answer:** *Does this help tell the story of digital infrastructure?* If not, remove it.

---

## 03 · Particle System Overview

One global engine. One simulation. Never reset.

**Particle DNA:** Position · Velocity · Acceleration · Mass · Rotation · Angular Velocity · Life · Glow · Depth · Temperature · Noise · Target · State · Energy

**Particle types:**

| Type | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Ambient | Background atmosphere |
| Signal | Represents customers/data |
| Construction | Builds objects in scene |
| Network | Creates connections |
| Cursor | Responds to mouse |
| Hero | Detached logo fragments |
| Energy | Powers transitions |
| Earth | Builds globe surface |
| Glow | Lighting support |

**Particle material:** Metallic shards · micro polygons · tiny triangles · crystal fragments · signal fragments. Never: snow, dust, stars, confetti, fireflies, smoke.

**Particle lifecycle:**
```
Birth → Travel → Assemble → Exist → Transform → Disassemble → Travel again
```

Particles never die. They change purpose.

---

## 04 · Particle Physics

Every particle contains:
- Mass, weight, momentum
- Drag, friction, inertia
- Attraction / repulsion to neighbors
- Velocity, acceleration

Particles influence each other. Nearby particles share velocity, attraction, and direction. The simulation feels organic, not random.

**Particle behavior states:**
```
Idle → Searching → Travelling → Connecting → Building → Living → Breaking → Returning
```

**Density by device:**
| Device | Count |
|--------|-------|
| Desktop | 3,000–8,000 |
| Tablet | 1,200–2,500 |
| Mobile | 400–1,200 |

Adjust dynamically. Performance always wins.

---

## 05 · Camera Language

The virtual camera behaves like cinema.

| Allowed | Prohibited |
|---------|------------|
| Slow zoom | Shake |
| Gentle orbit | Fast rotation |
| Perspective shifts | Whip pan |
| Soft parallax | Extreme zoom |
| Depth changes | Random movement |

Slow. Intentional. Elegant. Never aggressive. Camera movement is so subtle the visitor shouldn't consciously notice it.

---

## 06 · Cursor System

Cursor has invisible gravity (radius 120–240px).

**Effects on nearby elements:**
- Particles investigate, avoid, or orbit — then return naturally
- Background lighting reacts
- Cards tilt with perspective
- Buttons magnetize (snap slightly)
- Signals reroute around cursor

Nothing overreacts. Everything remains elegant.

---

## 07 · Timing Reference

| Interaction | Duration |
|-------------|----------|
| Micro (buttons, links) | 120–250ms |
| Hover states | 250–400ms |
| Section animations | 800–1,400ms |
| Major transformations | 1,500–3,000ms |

Motion breathes. Fast enough to feel responsive. Slow enough to feel cinematic.

---

## 08 · Transition Rules

Transitions never use opacity. Every section physically evolves into the next.

```
Hero            → logo matter becomes problem icons
Problem         → icons become signal paths
Growth System   → signal paths become engine cards
Growth Engines  → cards become screens
Portfolio       → screens become energy
Process         → energy becomes neural network
Intelligence    → network becomes Earth
CTA             → Earth becomes drifting particles (Footer)
```

Every transition preserves particle continuity. Matter is never created or destroyed — only transformed.

## 09 · Scene 01 — Hero

**Height:** 100vh · **Emotion:** Wonder · Confidence · Calm

**Purpose:** Create curiosity. Establish premium positioning. Introduce the living particle system.

**Layout:** Minimal. Massive logo. Large headline. Little text. Maximum negative space.

**Particle behavior:**
- Logo begins solid
- Only outer edges slowly erode (breathing cycle: 12–15s)
- Detached particles remain alive, travel slowly
- Never explode. Never disappear.

**Mouse:**
- Particles react softly
- Logo rotates slightly
- Background depth shifts

**Scroll trigger:**
- Logo edges erode fully
- Thousands of particles detach
- Particles flow downward
- Hero *becomes* Scene 02

---

## 10 · Scene 02 — The Problem

**Height:** 70vh · **Emotion:** Urgency · Recognition

**Purpose:** Surface broken business systems. Make the visitor feel recognized.

**Layout:** Huge typography. Minimal copy. Four large icons. No clutter.

**Particle behavior:**
- Incoming particles assemble four icons:
  - Ghosted Leads
  - Manual Chaos
  - Slow Response
  - Lost Revenue
- Icons physically construct themselves — never fade in
- No arrows. No bullets. Icons speak.

**Leaving:** Icons disassemble. Particles continue to Scene 03.

---

## 11 · Scene 03 — Growth System

**Height:** 120vh · **Emotion:** Understanding · Confidence

**Purpose:** Reveal the PYRAXIS ecosystem as one connected whole.

**Layout:** Massive circular ecosystem showing all eight engines connected.

```
Website → Lead → Booking → Follow-up → Retention → Review → Referral → Intelligence
```

**Particle behavior:**
- Signals continuously travel between nodes
- Hovering an engine redirects nearby particles toward it
- The ecosystem feels alive — signals never stop

**Mouse:** Hovering nodes creates particle focus. The visitor can feel the system respond.

**Leaving:** Particles disconnect from circular form. Flow into Scene 04.

---

## 12 · Scene 04 — Growth Engines

**Height:** 140vh · **Layout:** Horizontal storytelling

**Purpose:** Explain each engine in depth.

**Structure:** One reusable Engine component, displayed horizontally. Each engine builds itself on entry.

**Engine assembly sequence:**
```
Edges → Frame → Glass → Material → Icon → Typography → Signal activation → Data flow
```

Each engine has: own lighting · own animation variant · own particle behavior

**Horizontal scroll rule:** Only if usability is not compromised on mobile. Otherwise, vertical on mobile.

**Leaving:** Entire module dissolves. Particles continue.

---

## 13 · Scene 05 — Why PYRAXIS

**Height:** 80vh · **Emotion:** Calm · Human Connection

**Purpose:** Build trust through authenticity, not claims.

**Layout:** Editorial. Minimal. Large typography. No cards. Very little UI.

**Visual:** Particles create subtle geometric forms around copy. Lighting becomes softer. This section allows visual rest. The pacing slows intentionally after Scene 04's complexity.

**Leaving:** Geometric forms dissolve.

---

## 14 · Scene 06 — Portfolio / Deployments

**Height:** 120vh · **Emotion:** Trust · Capability

**Purpose:** Show real work. Not decoration.

**Layout:** Large immersive showcase. Projects construct themselves.

**Project assembly sequence:**
```
Frame → Glass → Display surface → UI elements → Typography
```

Avoid generic laptop mockups. If devices appear, they assemble piece by piece.

**Hover:** Soft depth shift · particle highlights · surface reflects

**Leaving:** Projects dissolve into particles.

---

## 15 · Scene 07 — Process

**Height:** 90vh · **Emotion:** Clarity

**Purpose:** Reduce uncertainty. Show what working with PYRAXIS looks like.

**Layout:** Vertical progression.

```
Discovery → Strategy → Build → Launch → Scale
```

No arrows. No boring timeline. The signal physically travels through each stage. The visitor follows it.

**Leaving:** Signal accelerates. Carries energy into Scene 08.

---

## 16 · Scene 08 — Intelligence Core

**Height:** 130vh · **Emotion:** Wonder · Spectacle

**Purpose:** The cinematic climax. Show PYRAXIS as living infrastructure at scale.

**Layout:** Massive open environment. No cards. Particles create:
- Neural systems
- Orbital paths
- Infrastructure networks
- Digital Earth
- Signal routes

**Mouse interaction (most interactive section):**
- Network reacts to cursor position
- Nodes respond to hover
- Signals reroute dynamically
- Earth slowly rotates
- Cursor pushes particles; they return naturally

**Leaving:** Network converges. Particles begin forming Earth shape.

---

## 17 · Scene 09 — CTA

**Height:** 100vh · **Emotion:** Confidence · Decision

**Purpose:** Conversion. The feeling should be inevitable, not aggressive.

**Layout:**
- Massive Earth (~30% visible, rising from bottom)
- Minimal copy above
- One primary button
- Particles orbit continuously

**Cursor:** Gently disturbs nearby particles. After leaving, they naturally return.

**Button:** The most premium interaction on the page. Hover triggers full button animation sequence. This is the moment of decision — make it feel earned.

---

## 18 · Scene 10 — Footer

**Height:** 40vh · **Emotion:** Silence · Calm

**Purpose:** Closure. The experience ends peacefully.

**Layout:** Minimal. Elegant.

Contents: Logo · Navigation · Contact · Copyright

**Particle behavior:** Slow drift. Almost no motion. The experience exhales. Never let the footer compete with anything.

---

## 19 · Visual Rhythm Map

Sections alternate in intensity intentionally:

| Scene | Intensity | Role |
|-------|-----------|------|
| Hero | Medium | Entry |
| Problem | High | Tension |
| Growth System | High | Discovery |
| Growth Engines | High | Exploration |
| Why PYRAXIS | **Low** | Rest |
| Portfolio | Medium | Proof |
| Process | Medium | Clarity |
| Intelligence Core | **Very High** | Climax |
| CTA | Medium | Conversion |
| Footer | **Very Low** | Closure |

Never let every section compete. Every scene has its own emotional role. The contrast between high and low intensity is what makes the climax feel earned.

---

## 20 · Responsive Experience

| Device | Adaptation |
|--------|------------|
| Desktop | Complete cinematic experience |
| Tablet | Reduced particle count, simplified camera, reduced blur |
| Mobile | No horizontal scroll if usability suffers, simplified transitions, lower density, touch replaces hover |

**Never simply shrink the desktop layout.** Mobile is its own intentionally designed experience. The story remains. The emotion remains. Only complexity reduces.

---

## 21 · Lighting Behavior

Lighting breathes — not flickers.

Per scene:
- Hero: High contrast, directional, logo-focused
- Problem: Cooler, slightly harsh (reinforces tension)
- Growth System: Warmer, signals glow brighter
- Intelligence Core: Maximum lighting complexity, Earth is brightest element
- Footer: Near-dark. Only ambient remains.

---

## 22 · Typography Animation

Typography never slides upward. Instead:

- Characters build from particles
- Light sweeps across headlines
- Mask reveals
- Tracking expands
- Weight changes

Letters should feel engineered. Every text entrance reinforces the "manufactured" quality of the brand.

---

## 23 · Icon Animation

Icons are manufactured. Every icon uses the same construction sequence:

```
Edges → Outline → Material → Glow → Final object
```

Icons dissolve back into particles when leaving a scene.

---

## 24 · Loading States

Never show spinners.

Instead:
- Particles assemble
- Skeleton states
- Signal animation
- Construction animation

Loading reinforces the brand. Even moments of waiting feel like PYRAXIS.

---

## 25 · The Experience Standard

The visitor should not remember individual sections.

They should remember one uninterrupted journey through a living digital infrastructure.

Every scene exists to make the next scene feel inevitable.

**The test:** Would someone leave this website unsure what PYRAXIS does but certain they've experienced something remarkable? If yes — the experience is working.
